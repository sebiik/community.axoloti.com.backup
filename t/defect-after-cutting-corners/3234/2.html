TheSlowGrowth 2017-04-19 19:16:00 UTC #2

The board is a four layer board - the two internal layers are used for power. They are very close together and tiny copper splices from the cutting can easily short them out. You can try to find shorts on the supply rails with a multimeter - I highly suspect that's the issue you're having there. If not - then I don't know.

If you find a short between the supply rails: You can of course try to fix it with very fine grid sandpaper (Sandpaper with grid no. 1000 or similar). You might be able to sand away the copper "bridge" that connected the layers. But even if you can remove the short, you might have damaged some components in the power supply.
