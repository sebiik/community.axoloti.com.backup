johannes 2016-02-09 22:08:31 UTC #2

Probably damaged in transport or during handling...

C46 is a "capacitor", part of the audio input circuitry.
Missing C46 will essentially cause degraded performance of the audio input.
It does not affect anything else than the audio input.

johannes 2016-02-09 22:24:58 UTC #4

The pads (where the capacitor is soldered on) have most likely peeled off the pcb substrate, that makes it impossible to solder it on the same place again.
Will require two "bodge wires" to reconstruct the capacitor, and some soldering skills.
A half-fix - restoring some of the audio input quality is connecting the "LIN+" pad to the "CM" pad, without the capacitor. Will still have reduced ground loop immunity on the audio input.
