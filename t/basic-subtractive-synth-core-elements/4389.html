After working through the tutorials and reading the Axoloti book @janvantomme I came up with my first synth.

It's architecture is quite basic and loosely based on the TB303 and sounds familiar.

My main goals in creating this synth were to get a better understanding of the Axoloti an how to create some functions elementary to subtractive synths.

The following features are included:

  • mixing OSC-waveforms
  • modulating a filter with an envelope with varying amount
  • filter key tracking with amount
  • Legato glide with glide time control
  • Pitchbend with range control
  • applying several modulation sources to one modulation target (PB+glide->OSC-Pitch and Env-Amount+Key-Track-Amount->Filter-Pitch)
  • two switchable "Amp"-Channels: clean and distortion
  • mapping Midi CC#01 (Mod-Wheel) to the envelope amount control to the filter

Maybe this patch is interesting to other new Axoloti users...so I am happy to share it.
