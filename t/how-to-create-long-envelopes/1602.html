johannes 2016-04-29 00:46:40 UTC #3

The env/ahd has a bit of a weird parameter scaling, something I'd like to fix in the future, but the puzzle is avoiding to break existing patches.
The A/D parameters can be adjusted in smaller than 0.5 unit increments by mouse if you hold shift (0.05 unit increments) or even finer if you hold ctrl-shift (0.01 units). You can also make more precise adjustments by entering a value on the keyboard (like 63.995) followed by enter to accept (press escape to cancel). Only 2 digits of the dial position will be shown, but more will be used (and saved). At a certain point you'll also bump into numerical precision errors, and the time corresponding to the dial position shown in the object will no longer correspond.

Like say an AHD with an attack of 2 minutes, hold of 15 minutes, decay of 5 minutes.

Just a note, the Axoloti's AHD is actually a misnamed AR envelope, so for a hold time of 15 minutes you'd just need a gate that stays high for 17 minutes (2 minutes added for the attack) and the decay only comes after the gate goes low (which I would consider a release). I'm not sure if there is an actual AHD in the sense I would mean by it (with a hold time parameter and a decay that starts after the hold time is finished regardless of the gate).
