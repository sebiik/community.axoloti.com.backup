Inaba 2016-05-18 01:08:16 UTC #1

i ordered 3 new axoloti for a specific project, namely my massive lead synth.
it's target is to have a cheaper alternative to the big stuff (example: access virus series, clavia nord lead series) while having an 'old-school' feeling by a wooden case.

featuring 3 axoloti boards (sound processing) and 2 teensy boards this will be a powerbeast.
3 octave keyboard, ili9341 display, rotary encoders and more.
all will be housed in a wooden enclosure, ~65cm wide, ~70mm high, ~250mm deep

"so, why 3 axoloti and 2 teensy boards?"
simple answer: load balancing and thus more performance, see my sketch below on how i want to do this:

axo #1:
oscillators
midi = CC controls from teensy #1 (serial1)
midi out (forwarded from teensy #2)
usb = midi notes from teensy #2

axo #2:
main FX
midi = CC controls from teensy #1 (serial2)

axo #3:
reverb
main out
midi = CC controls from teensy #1 (serial3)

teensy #1:
control params (5x rotary encoders)
ili9341 display (to see what you're adjusting)

teensy #2:
midi keyboard matrix (hopefully with velocity!)
midi pitchbend/modulation

in depth specs:
5 voice polyvoice with each:
- 2 supersaws, 4 square oscillators
- lowpass filter with envelope
- highpass filter with keytracking
- pitchbend
- panning (applies to the lowest and highest square and both supersaws)
1 global sine lfo
my own distortion module (one left, one right)
my own 'soundgood module' which can be toggled to compare the added warmth (one left, one right)
my own stereo width mix (not released yet)
dual mono delay with lowpass filter
massive stereo reverb (thanks kausto for the patch!)

will update as much as possible.
