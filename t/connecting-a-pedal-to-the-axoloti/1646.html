kassu 2016-05-06 08:21:21 UTC #2

Do you have a pedal already that you want to connect?

Most pedals, as far as I know, are either an on-off switch, or a potentiometer. Either of those is easy to hook up to axolotis I/O pins directly. It will require soldering some wires on the Axoloti, and connect them to a jack socket, but it's easy work.

If you can share what kind of pedal you have in mind, I can probably give some instructions how to wire it up.

janvantomme 2016-05-06 13:26:51 UTC #5

I worked on connecting the foot pedal of my old Behringer V-Amp this afternoon. The only thing you need to solder to the Axoloti is a Neutrik stereo jack input. You can get these for around €1.

Photo over here:

twitter.com

Jan Vantomme (vormplus)

Connected my Behringer V-Amp foot pedal to the Axoloti. This will be fun for looper + overdub patches.
06:08 - 6 mei 2016

Connections:
Sleeve to VDD
Ring to PB7
Tip to PB6

The patch uses to gpio/in/digital objects, mode needs to be set to pulldown.
