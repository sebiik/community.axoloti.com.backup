POWER from Eurorack to Axoloti

[Answered] Powering Axoloti from Eurorack

You should connect Axoloti to +12V and ground (0V). Avoid using -12V. Also take in account the power consuption, If I remember correctly Axoloti alone consumes about 100mA, but if you want to connect something at the USB port, the consuption could rise up to 500mA, wich may be too much for your Eurorack Power Supply.

Eurorack power connection

First you won't be able to fit a Axo board into 4-6HP unless you break off the MIDI section and even that will be a tight fit with some euro rails (mainly Z-rails), As far as the power goes though, that is simple, the Axo takes between 7-15V so the +12V of the Eurorack will be no problem. I'll share my simple solution. It's not exactly eurorack, because my system is banana, but otherwise this is basically a DIY Eurorack system.

Here are the two Axoloti modules in my system. 6 assignable analog pots, two assignable buttons, two analog ins, two analog outs. I cut the boards and sandwiched them. Also desoldered all the big clunky jacks. Power is coming directly from my 12v rail into the Axo -- just soldered a cable to the power input.

IMG_4320.jpg3264x2448 1.35 MB

This USB port is on the back of the case. It's only connected to one module at the moment (midi and micro device port), but based on whatever I'm working on, I sometimes connect both ports to MIDI, or both to micro-device.

IMG_4321.jpg3264x2448 1.37 MB

My analog inputs are protected with voltage dividers and the traditional clipping diode circuit (like the image below -- posted by @haK in an old discussion). The outputs aren't scaled up, and I haven't found the need to boost them.

Everything has been working really well for a while now.

JonasBers 2018-08-07 22:51:51 UTC #7

@widdly, attenuating and boosting is fine, although it doesn't provide protection, so if you manage to send in a hot enough signal, it will fry the input regardless of the attenuation. as stated, I use a combination of attenuation to limit distortion (voltage divider, aka series resistor followed by resistor to ground) and clipping diodes. if anything larger than 3.3v and less than 12v enters the input, it will be clipped at 3.3v and remain safe.
