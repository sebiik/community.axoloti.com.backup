johannes 2016-01-19 14:51:34 UTC #3

The parameters have a different "taper" (how the position relates to the level).
Square scaling is more usable for audio level than linear scaling.
Linear scaling for a level control has little (only 6dB) effect in the top half of the parameter.
Square scaling doubles that to 12dB of attenuation in the top half.
It's not log scale - that would be the same knob travel for a certain number of decibels, but also on mixer desk faders you will see that the number of decibels per centimeter increase near the bottom. Square taper has similar properties.
