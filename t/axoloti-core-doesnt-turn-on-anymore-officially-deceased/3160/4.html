johannes 2017-08-21 17:17:53 UTC #15

I recall doing a post-mortem analysis remotely on a board with a heating up U4/U5, and it appeared that those heating up was not a cause of failure but a consequence. After eliminating U4/U5 and applying power, it was found that the microcontroller (IC2) itself started to heat up instead. The event that caused the microcontroller to fail was impossible to identify, but the hypothesis was that a flying, exposed power supply wire carrying over 6V temporarily made contact with a GPIO pad.
So, yes it is probably dead...
