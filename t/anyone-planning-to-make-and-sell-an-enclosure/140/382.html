Naviow 2018-08-07 14:08:18 UTC #380

Hi Ghostly, the finger plates come as 300mm x 150mm x 1.3mm thick, they can be cut in half with multiple 'scores' from a craft knife then snapped. They are really common and can be bought from DIY store etc but I got mine from Ebay here:

ebay.co.uk

Push Door Metal FINGER PLATE 300 x 75 mm Aluminium Brass Stainless Steel Copper ...

Door Finger / Push Plates. In a hurry?. Available 300 x 75 mm - 12" x 3". | eBay!

be aware that some have rounded edges which i didn't want and there are some odd sizes advertised so I bought enough for the whole case (and a spare ) just in case).

1.3mm is fine for my small 'modules' but the longer 300mm ones are a bit on the thin side - I dont know if you have seen 'Look Mum No Computer' on Youtube ? he makes his own modules out of 2mm aluminum (and sprays them black) which would be better if you can find it. Another option I looked into was copper clad pcb board - might be an option
john

here is mine:

IMG_20180816_202950-1.jpg2387x1689 1.42 MB

i wanted a compact pedal, so this is standard 1590BB Hammond pedal size. the holes are all made with a drill, a fretsaw and some filing. the "artwork" is a newspaper image and the "sparkling" topping in the lacquer is powder from the aluminium filing. had to break the axoloti apart of course. it is a tight fit, but it works. as of now it is a midi clock generator, selects different lfo waveforms on a connected blofeld and acts as a talkbox sound source and fx. (audio mono out to talkbox, from microphone to audio in, processing, audio out mono to mixer) yeah!! thanks to @SmashedTransistors for the OLED object.
