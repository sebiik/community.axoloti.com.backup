janvantomme 2016-01-15 14:17:42 UTC #141

The STL files are in mm. Easiest way to see is to use the measuring tool Meshlab http://sourceforge.net/projects/meshlab/files/meshlab/MeshLab%20v1.3.3/

Material: PLA (Biodegradable plastic) or ABS (Lego Plastic). Both are cheap and good for technical parts.
100 microns will give you the best looking result, but might be a bit more expensive since it will take longer to print.

Jens_Groh 2016-01-15 15:12:54 UTC #142

You can also view with Netfabb Basic (free). Or Sketchup, it can import STL. (Maybe only with a plug-in installed? I don't remember.) BTW, I use the free Sketchup version, it's quite okay for basic 3D print design.
A not-too-expensive print service here in Germany is fabberhouse.de, don't know if they are good.
I printed mine in PLA with (luxury ) 0.09mm layers. I'd recommend heated print bed and/or glue to avoid warping, I had slight problems.
Cool idea that stack case. Maybe for "shields", too, if we interpret the Axoloti I/O ports as a kind of bus system.

There are few bugs in my design so I leave it to you to fix it:
1) the front panel (panel with outputs, usb connection, etc.) should be 2 mm and in my design it is 3 mm, but all holes around front panel are for 2 mm design. So basically you need to make holes wider or to make front panel on 2 mm thick plate.
2) at the bottom plate there are 4 holes to support axoloti core. 2 holes are closer to front panel and their location is correct, but 2 others holes are not at correct location.

This bugs are not critical, I spotted them quite late but I found workaround.
So here is my design http://14circles.org/axoloti/axi1.svg
This file is created in Inkscape and can be used directly in http://www.formulor.de/ shop.

Enjoy.
