ricard 2023-01-24 23:30:06 UTC #2

"Real" aftertouch, i.e. based on the MIDI spec, is not a CC, it's a separate MIDI message (actually, two different ones, depending on whether we're talking about channel aftertouch or polyphonic aftertouch; in general when we talk about aftertouch we mean the former as few keyboards had polyphonic aftertouch, but I've noticed lately that a couple of newer high end synths do have poly aftertouch), on the other hand CC 131 doesn't make sense as there are only 128 of them.

It sounds though that the Axiom can be reconfigured to transmit its aftertouch information in a variety of ways, so assuming "CC 131" means it sends an aftertouch message, it might be possible to reconfigure it to send a normal CC instead and use that in the Axoloti. Or perhaps "CC 131" means something else than an aftertouch message in which case it should be possible to reconfigure appropriately.
