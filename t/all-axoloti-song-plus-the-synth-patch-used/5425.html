Nerd notes:

I first made the drum patterns in my axoloti sample based drum machine, then recorded audio whilst I jammed with the patterns (kick on one track, snare and hats on the other). Then I fed the audio through my live sampler/filter patch a couple of times, recording audio while I played around with knobs/triggers.

The patches were the ones on this thread-

3 axoloti guitar/bass/drum machine setup Your Music/Projects
Thought I'd share the jam set up I've been working on for the last couple of months. Still tweaking it, but it's coming together nicely. It's 3 axolotis running: Sample based drum machine with a 64 step sequencer with quantized live midi recording. 2x audio loopers, each with it's own reverb and it's own synced delay with filter and time modulation. One Looper also goes through 4 filters (2 lo pass and 2 hi) which are triggered via midi, cut off is controlled by note. It also goes throuâ€¦

Then, I recorded the midi for the keyboard parts on the laptop, did a little tidying and fed the midi back through the synth linked above. I did this multiple times for each synth part, sending the same midi through different synth settings and playing with the knobs whilst recording.

Then I mixed the resulting audio, with a bit of compression/eq here and there. Put a master limiter on, and it was done.

Hope you like it!
