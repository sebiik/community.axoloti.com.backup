axoman 2019-09-06 21:44:17 UTC #2

Ah that's easy, my fave object is sss/dyn/axoComp which is a special compressor Remco custom made for me, and even named after me

Built upon what was already a banging compressor to start with, at my request he added custom curve control to the attack and release. It means you can dial-in logarithmic, linear, inverse logarithmic, or anything inbetween, independently for both the attack and release of the compressor, giving more punch, and a lot of control over the character of the compressor as a whole.

Personally I've spent an embarrasing amount of time putting my vinyl collection through it, and have really enjoyed doing so, it's a great way to master compression technique and can be very addictive!

borututuforte 2019-09-07 09:58:10 UTC #4

Some of my favourites at the moment are:

  1. tiar/mux/smux4_1 and similiar "smux" objects - super cool to get smooth transitions between (more than two) otherwise seperated signals. I've got a nice patch where I use two of these with four (pseudo-?) physical modelling braids-oscillators as inputs.

  2. sss/math/expCurveK - I like to put envelopes into this so I can live tweak their snappiness, something not many synths do. There's also an audio rate version (expCurveA) which can do some interesting waveshaping.

  3. sss/delay/loop4 - I somehow didn't get rbrts looper objects and this one just worked. It gave me a four track looper with functioning MIDI-sync, so I could sell my overpriced Pigtronix looper pedal.
